Why Session Opens Matter in ICT

ICT methodology places enormous weight on session opens as reference points for price delivery. The London Open (3 AM EST), New York Open (8 AM EST), and Midnight Open are not just time markers ‚Äî they're the price levels around which institutional order flow is orchestrated. Liquidity sweeps, fake-outs, and displacement moves are disproportionately clustered around these opens.

Hourly opens extend this logic across the full trading day. Every hour's opening price is a potential reference level. This indicator automates the plotting of all session and hourly opens, kill zones, and the Asian range ‚Äî giving you the complete ICT time-based framework without manual line drawing.

Midnight Open

The Midnight Open is one of the most important reference levels in ICT. It divides the trading day into two 12-hour periods and acts as a magnetic price level. The indicator plots it with a distinct line and can extend it to the following midnight.
